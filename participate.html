Participate In Research

Below is a list of online studies where we are currently collecting data. To participate in this research, click the links below.

If you are a University of Stirling student participating for tokens, please sign-up for these studies via the PsychWeb portal.

Perceptions of names.

We are running a study investigating social perception of names. This study should take approx. 10 minutes. Click here to participate.

Online dating and user mental health.

Jess De La Mare, as part of her PhD, is investigating how online dating use influences mental health. This study should take approx. 20 minutes. Click here to participate.

What makes a face attractive?

We are interested in factors that influence facial attractiveness judgements. This study should take approx. 10 minutes. Click here to participate.

Judgements of trustworthiness from faces.

Freya Benson, as part of their undergraduate dissertation, is investigating factors that influence the trustworthiness of faces. This study should take no longer than 10 minutes. Click here to participate.

Is physical attractiveness associated with performance in tennis players?

Erika Campbell, as part of their undergraduate dissertation, is investigating facial attraction in a sporting context. This study should take approximately 10 minutes to complete. Click here to participate.

Investigating the factors that influence parasocial relationships.

Taylor Gordon, as part of their undergraduate dissertation, is investigating factors that influence parasocial relationships. This study should take approximately 10 minutes. Click here to participate.

Rating prospective online dating bios.

As part of their undergraduate dissertation, Aimee Paterson is interested factors that influence perceptions of online dating bios. This study should take approximately 5 minutes. Click here to participate.

The effect of gender on information seeking in individuals with chronic pain.

Sian Tyreman is investigating how those with chronic pain seek information online as part of their undergraduate dissertation project. This study should take approximately 10 minutes. Click here to participate.
